Hardware Fault Troubleshooting

1. POST & Beep Codes (Startup Errors)

When a computer fails to boot, the BIOS Power-On Self-Test (POST) may emit beep codes to identify the faulty component.

Common Beep Codes (AMI/Award BIOS)

Beep Pattern Likely Faulty Component Action
1 Long, 2 Short Video Card (GPU) Reseat GPU, check power cables.
Continuous Short Beeps Memory (RAM) Reseat RAM, try one stick at a time.
5 Short Beeps Processor (CPU) Check CPU seating and cooling fan.
No Beeps + No Fan Spin Power Supply (PSU) Check power cable, test PSU voltage.

Correction Note: While some older systems might signal DRAM with "Long + Short", the standard for modern BIOS is that 1 Long + 2 Short indicates a Graphics (GPU) error, not RAM.

2. Drive & Boot Configuration

Errors occurring after the BIOS screen usually relate to storage devices.

SATA/HDD Errors

Symptom: "Select Boot Device" or "Boot Device Not Found".

  • Cause: The HDD is detected but has no OS, or the cable is loose.
  • Fix: Check SATA data/power cables. Ensure the correct drive is first in the BIOS Boot Order.
  • Status: Non-Fatal (System has power, just can't load Windows).

Legacy IDE Configuration

Symptom: CD-ROM or secondary HDD not showing up.

  • Context: Older IDE (PATA) ribbons support two devices: a Master and a Slave.
  • Fix: Check the Jumpers on the back of the drive. If two devices share a cable, one must be Master and the other Slave.

3. Power Supply Unit (PSU) Check

If the PC is completely dead (No fans, no lights), the PSU is the prime suspect.

The "Paperclip Test" (Jump Start)

To test a PSU without a motherboard:

  1. Unplug the PSU from the wall.
  2. Locate the large 24-pin motherboard connector.
  3. Find the Green Wire (PS_ON) and any Black Wire (Ground).
  4. Bridge them with a paperclip or wire.
  5. Plug the PSU back into the wall.

Result: If the PSU fan spins, it is likely turning on. If not, the PSU is dead.

Voltage Checks

If the system powers on but is unstable (e.g., random restarts), check the voltages:

  • Yellow Wire: +12V (CPU/GPU Power).
  • Red Wire: +5V (Disk drives/logic).
  • Orange Wire: +3.3V (M.2 drives/RAM).

4. Expansion Cards (PCI/PCIe)

Issues with modems, network cards, or sound cards.

  • Symptom: Device not detected in Device Manager.
  • Status: Non-Fatal. The PC will still boot.
  • Fix: Move the card to a different PCI slot to rule out a bad motherboard slot.
